Belém Tower

← Back to Castle Index

Overview

Country: Portugal

Location: Lisbon, Portugal

Architectural Style: Manueline (Portuguese Late Gothic)

Year Built: 1514-1520

Built to defend Lisbon's harbor and commemorate Vasco da Gama's expedition, this elegant tower represents the Manueline style at its finest. The tower served as both fortress and ceremonial gateway to Lisbon during Portugal's Age of Discovery.

Key Features

  • Manueline architectural masterpiece
  • Built to defend Lisbon harbor
  • Commemorates Age of Discovery
  • Elegant limestone construction
  • Maritime motifs in decoration
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983
