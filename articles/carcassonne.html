Overview

Country: France

Location: Carcassonne, Aude, Occitanie

Architectural Style: Medieval fortified city

Year Built: 1850

The fortified city of Carcassonne is a medieval marvel with its double ring of ramparts and 53 towers creating an imposing silhouette. Restored by Eugène Viollet-le-Duc in the 19th century, this UNESCO World Heritage site represents one of France's most complete examples of medieval fortification.

Key Features

  • Double ring of ramparts
  • 53 towers and turrets
  • Medieval fortified city
  • Viollet-le-Duc restoration
  • Château Comtal within the walls
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1997
