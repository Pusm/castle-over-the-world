Château de Chantilly

← Back to Castle Index

Overview

Country: France

Location: Chantilly, Oise, Hauts-de-France

Architectural Style: Renaissance and 19th-century reconstruction

Year Built: 1300-1400

Home to the Duke of Aumale and containing the second-largest collection of antique paintings in France after the Louvre, Chantilly combines Renaissance architecture with 19th-century reconstruction. The château is famous for its magnificent stables and art collections.

Key Features

  • Second-largest art collection in France
  • Magnificent Great Stables
  • Renaissance and 19th-century architecture
  • Home of Duke of Aumale
  • Condé Museum
  • Beautiful French formal gardens
