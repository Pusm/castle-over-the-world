Overview

Country: Scotland

Location: Stirling, Scotland

Architectural Style: Renaissance palace and medieval fortress

Year Built: 1101-1200

Known as the 'Key to Scotland' due to its strategic position, Stirling Castle has witnessed some of Scotland's most pivotal moments including the coronation of Mary Queen of Scots. The castle combines medieval fortification with one of Scotland's finest Renaissance palaces.

Key Features
