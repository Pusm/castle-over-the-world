Spiš Castle

Overview

Country: Slovakia

Location: Spišské Podhradie, Slovakia

Architectural Style: Romanesque and Gothic

Year Built: 1101-1200

One of the largest castle complexes in the world, Spiš Castle spreads over 4 hectares atop a travertine hill. This UNESCO World Heritage site represents 800 years of architectural development and served as an important center of power in medieval Hungary.

Key Features

  • One of largest castle complexes worldwide
  • Spreads over 4 hectares
  • Built on travertine hill
  • 800 years of architectural development
  • Important center of medieval Hungary
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1993
