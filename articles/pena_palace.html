Overview

Country: Portugal

Location: Sintra, Portugal

Architectural Style: Romantic Revival (Gothic, Renaissance, Moorish, Manueline)

Year Built: 1842-1854

One of Europe's most fantastical palaces, Pena Palace crowns the hills of Sintra with its colorful romantic architecture blending Gothic, Renaissance, Moorish, and Manueline styles. Built for King Ferdinand II, it represents the Portuguese romantic movement and showcases architectural eclecticism.

Key Features

  • Colorful romantic architecture
  • Blend of multiple architectural styles
  • Built for King Ferdinand II
  • Crowns hills of Sintra
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Romantic movement masterpiece
