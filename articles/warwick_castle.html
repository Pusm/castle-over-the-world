Warwick Castle

Warwick, Warwickshire, England

1068 (William the Conqueror era)

Overview

Built by William the Conqueror in 1068, Warwick Castle exemplifies the evolution from Norman military fortress to stately home. This well-preserved medieval castle combines formidable defensive architecture with later residential improvements, showcasing nearly a thousand years of English castle development and aristocratic life.

Basic Information

Architectural Style:
Norman Medieval with later additions
Construction Period:
1068 (William the Conqueror era)
Location:
Warwick, Warwickshire, England

Key Features & Highlights

  • Great Hall with medieval weaponry displays
  • 14th-century towers and battlements
  • Dungeon with torture chamber
  • State rooms with period furnishings
  • Working trebuchet (largest in the world)
  • Peacock Garden and conservatory
