Overview

Country: United Kingdom

Location: Warwick, Warwickshire, England

Architectural Style: Norman Medieval with Georgian and Victorian additions

Year Built: 1068

Built by William the Conqueror in 1068, Warwick Castle exemplifies the evolution from Norman military fortress to stately home. Known as the 'Kingmaker's Castle' after Richard Neville, it represents the pinnacle of medieval English fortress evolution and has been central to English politics for nearly a millennium.

Key Features

  • Great Hall with medieval weaponry displays and 62-foot length
  • Caesar's Tower - 147 feet high with historic prison cells
  • Guy's Tower - 14th-century defensive masterpiece
  • World's largest working trebuchet (18m tall, 22 tonnes)
  • Watergate Tower (Ghost Tower) - restored by Anthony Salvin
  • State Rooms with period furnishings and Cedar Drawing Room
  • Capability Brown landscaped grounds covering 690 acres
  • Medieval dungeon complex with torture chamber displays
