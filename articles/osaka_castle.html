Overview

Country: Japan

Location: Osaka, Kansai region

Architectural Style: Japanese castle (reconstructed)

Year Built: 1583

One of Japan's most famous castles, Osaka Castle was originally built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi as the centerpiece of his campaign to unify Japan. The current ferro-concrete reconstruction houses a modern museum while maintaining the castle's iconic five-story, eight-level structure with its distinctive green roof and golden ornaments.

Key Features

  • Built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi
  • Five-story, eight-level structure
  • Distinctive green roof and golden ornaments
  • Modern museum inside
  • Beautiful park setting
  • Symbol of Japanese unification
