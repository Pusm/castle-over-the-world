Overview

Country: Wales

Location: Caerphilly, South Wales

Architectural Style: 13th-century concentric castle

Year Built: 1268-1271

Sprawling over 30 acres, Caerphilly Castle is the largest castle in Wales and the second-largest in Britain. This masterpiece of medieval military engineering features an elaborate system of moats and fortifications, with its leaning tower that out-leans Pisa's famous tower.

Key Features

  • Largest castle in Wales
  • Second-largest castle in Britain
  • Elaborate moat and fortification system
  • Famous leaning tower
  • Concentric castle design
  • Water defense system
