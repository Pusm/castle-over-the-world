Overview

Country: India

Location: Mysore, Karnataka

Architectural Style: Indo-Saracenic architecture

Year Built: 1912

Known as the 'Palace of Palaces,' Mysore Palace is a magnificent example of Indo-Saracenic architecture combining Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic styles. The palace of the Wadiyar dynasty is famous for its illumination during Dussehra festival when 97,000 light bulbs transform it into a golden spectacle.

Key Features

  • 97,000 light bulbs illumination
  • Magnificent Durbar Hall
