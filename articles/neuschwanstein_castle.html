Neuschwanstein Castle

← Back to Castle Index

Overview

Country: Germany

Location: Bavarian Alps, near Füssen

Architectural Style: Romantic Revival (Gothic Revival, Romanesque, Byzantine)

Year Built: 1869-1886

A fairy-tale castle commissioned by King Ludwig II of Bavaria, Neuschwanstein embodies 19th-century Romantic ideals with its blend of medieval architectural styles. Despite contemporary critics dismissing it as 'kitsch,' it stands today as one of Europe's most iconic examples of historicism and has inspired countless fairy tale adaptations.

Key Features

  • Double-helix staircase design
  • Throne Hall with Byzantine décor
  • Gothic Revival towers and spires
  • Advanced 19th-century technical achievements
  • Romanesque geometric forms
  • Inspiration for Disney's Sleeping Beauty Castle
