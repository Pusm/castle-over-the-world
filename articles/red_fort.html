Overview

Country: India

Location: Delhi, India

Architectural Style: Mughal architecture

Year Built: 1639-1648

Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as the fortified palace of Shahjahanabad, the Red Fort served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. Its red sandstone walls and sophisticated Mughal architecture represent the zenith of Indo-Islamic architectural achievement.

Key Features

  • Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan
  • Main residence of Mughal emperors
  • Red sandstone walls
  • Sophisticated Mughal architecture
  • Diwan-i-Aam and Diwan-i-Khas
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2007
