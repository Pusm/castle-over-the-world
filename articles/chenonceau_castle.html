Château de Chenonceau

← Back to Castle Index

Overview

Country: France

Location: Chenonceaux, Indre-et-Loire, Loire Valley

Architectural Style: French Renaissance

Year Built: 1514-1522

Known as the 'Ladies' Castle' due to the influential women who shaped its history, Chenonceau spans the River Cher with its distinctive arched bridge gallery. This elegant château represents the height of French Renaissance architecture and is the most visited château in France after Versailles.

Key Features

  • Spans the River Cher with arched bridge
  • Known as the 'Ladies' Castle'
  • French Renaissance masterpiece
  • Most visited château after Versailles
  • Famous gallery over the river
  • Rich history of influential women owners
