Overview

Country: Japan

Location: Kumamoto, Kyushu

Architectural Style: Japanese castle

Year Built: 1467

Considered one of Japan's three premier castles alongside Osaka and Nagoya, Kumamoto Castle is renowned for its sophisticated defensive design and curved stone walls called 'musha-gaeshi' that prevented enemy climbing. The castle offers spectacular 360-degree views from its top floors.

Key Features

  • One of Japan's three premier castles
  • Curved stone walls (musha-gaeshi)
  • Sophisticated defensive design
  • 360-degree panoramic views
  • Earthquake-resistant construction
  • Important Cultural Property of Japan
