Overview

Country: United Kingdom

Location: Windsor, Berkshire, England

Architectural Style: Georgian and Victorian design on Medieval structure

Year Built: 1001-1100

The world's oldest occupied castle, Windsor embodies nearly a millennium of architectural evolution. Originally built after the Norman Conquest, it seamlessly blends Classical, Gothic, and Rococo styles within its medieval framework, serving as both a royal residence and a symbol of British monarchy through the ages.

Key Features

  • Britain's oldest crown jewels (Honours of Scotland)
  • Georgian state apartments
  • Gothic features in modern interpretation
  • Jacobethan elements
  • Rococo decorative schemes
  • Finest expression of later Georgian taste
