Overview

Country: United States

Location: East Haddam, Connecticut

Architectural Style: Medieval-inspired eclectic

Year Built: 1914-1919

This zany, medieval-inspired fortress was dreamed up by William Gillette, famous playwright and Sherlock Holmes actor. Built as his retirement home, the castle features 47 unique doors, secret passages, hidden rooms, and thousands of hand-crafted details that reflect Gillette's theatrical personality.

Key Features

  • 47 unique doors (no two identical)
  • Secret doors and hidden passages
  • Hand-carved wooden fixtures
  • Built-in furniture and couches
  • Hidden mirror spy system
  • 200-acre surrounding park
