Overview

Country: Germany

Location: Schwerin, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern

Architectural Style: Romantic Historicism

Year Built: 1845

Known as the 'Neuschwanstein of the North,' Schwerin Castle sits on a small island in Lake Schwerin. This fairy-tale palace serves as the seat of the Mecklenburg-Vorpommern state parliament and showcases 19th-century romantic castle architecture at its finest.

Key Features

  • Known as 'Neuschwanstein of the North'
  • Built on island in Lake Schwerin
  • Seat of state parliament
  • Fairy-tale romantic architecture
  • 365 towers and turrets
  • Beautiful palace gardens
