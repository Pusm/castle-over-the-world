The Alhambra

Granada, Andalusia, Spain

1238-1358

Overview

The Alhambra stands as the finest example of Moorish architecture in the Western world, representing the sophisticated artistic achievement of Islamic Spain. Built by the Nasrid dynasty, this palace-fortress complex showcases intricate geometric patterns, muqarnas vaulting, and garden courtyards that influenced architectural traditions across the Mediterranean.

Basic Information

Architectural Style:
Moorish (Islamic) Architecture
Construction Period:
1238-1358
Location:
Granada, Andalusia, Spain

Engineering & Technical Analysis

Foundation Engineering

Geological Base:
Granada plateau constructed using rammed earth (tapial) foundation system
Formation Process:
Muros de tapial construction compressing soil mixed with aggregates, lime and water into formwork

Engineering Advantages

  • Rammed earth construction provides excellent thermal mass for climate control
  • Elevated plateau position commands strategic views over Granada valley
  • Natural defensive position protected by steep cliffs and ravines
  • Tapial construction allows for rapid, cost-effective large-scale building

Construction Techniques

Hydraulic Engineering

  • Sultan's Canal (Saqiyat al-Sultan) brings mountain water to plateau via sophisticated elevation devices
  • Internal network of stone-lined acequias (canals) for water distribution throughout complex
  • Qanāt-like conduits and albercones (cisterns) for water storage and redirection
  • Integrated fountain and pool systems providing climate control and acoustic effects

Structural Systems

  • Rectangular courtyard units with central water features as basic architectural module
  • Arcaded porticoes flanking courtyards on two or four sides
  • Slender columns supporting delicate muqarnas vaulting systems
  • Precise geometric planning integrating decorative and structural elements

Material Properties & Engineering

RammedEarth:
Tapial construction provides excellent thermal mass, earthquake flexibility, and rapid construction capability
GypsumPlaster:
Smooth decorative surface enabling intricate carved geometric patterns and Arabic calligraphy
Ceramics:
Glazed tile work provides waterproofing and decorative geometric patterns resistant to weathering
PineTimber:
Lightweight roofing material with natural insect resistance suitable for Mediterranean climate

Key Features & Highlights

  • Intricate Islamic geometric patterns
  • Court of Lions with fountain system
  • Muqarnas (stalactite) vaulting
  • Arabic calligraphy decorations
  • Palatial gardens and water features
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
