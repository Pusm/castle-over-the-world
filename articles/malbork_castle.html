Malbork Castle

Malbork, Pomeranian Voivodeship, Poland

13th century (1274-1457)

Overview

The world's largest castle by land area and Europe's largest brick castle, Malbork was built by the Teutonic Knights as their headquarters. This massive red brick complex served as the center of the Teutonic Order's power in the Baltic region and stands as the ultimate example of medieval brick construction techniques.
