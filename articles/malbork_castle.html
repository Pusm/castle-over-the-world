Overview

Country: Poland

Location: Malbork, Pomeranian Voivodeship

Architectural Style: Gothic brick architecture

Year Built: 1200-1300

The world's largest castle by land area and Europe's largest brick castle, Malbork was built by the Teutonic Knights as their headquarters. This massive red brick complex served as the center of the Teutonic Order's power in the Baltic region and stands as the ultimate example of medieval brick construction techniques.

Key Features

  • Largest castle in the world by land area
  • Largest brick castle in Europe
  • Eight massive towers and high curtain walls
  • Gothic brick architecture masterpiece
  • Former headquarters of Teutonic Knights
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1997
