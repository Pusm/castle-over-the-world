Overview

Country: China

Location: Beijing, China

Architectural Style: Chinese imperial architecture

Year Built: 1406

The imperial palace complex that served as home to 24 Ming and Qing dynasty emperors for over 500 years. Covering 720,000 square meters with 980 buildings, it's the world's most-visited palace and represents the pinnacle of traditional Chinese palatial architecture.

Key Features

  • Home to 24 emperors across 500 years
  • 720,000 square meters (180 acres)
  • 980 buildings and structures
  • World's most-visited palace
  • Traditional Chinese imperial architecture
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1987
