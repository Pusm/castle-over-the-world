Overview

Country: United States

Location: Pollepel Island, Hudson River, New York

Architectural Style: Scottish-style fortress

Year Built: 1901-1918

Built by arms dealer Francis Bannerman VI as an ammunition storage facility and summer residence, this Scottish-style castle with turrets, towers, and a moat was largely destroyed by fire but remains a haunting ruin accessible only by boat tours through the Hudson River.

Key Features

  • Scottish-style turrets and towers
  • Original moat system
  • Arms and ammunition storage history
  • Dramatic fire-damaged ruins
  • Hudson River island location
  • Accessible only by boat
