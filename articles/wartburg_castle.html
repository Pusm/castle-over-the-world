Overview

Country: Germany

Location: Eisenach, Thuringia

Architectural Style: Romanesque

Year Built: 1067

A UNESCO World Heritage site of outstanding cultural significance, Wartburg Castle is where Martin Luther translated the New Testament and where the legendary Sängerkrieg (Contest of Minnesingers) supposedly took place. This castle embodies nearly a thousand years of German history and culture.

Key Features

  • Where Martin Luther translated New Testament
  • Site of legendary Sängerkrieg
  • Nearly 1000 years of German history
  • Romanesque architecture
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1999
