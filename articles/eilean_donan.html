Overview

Country: Scotland

Location: Kyle of Lochalsh, Highland

Architectural Style: Scottish baronial (20th-century reconstruction)

Year Built: 1200-1300

One of the most photographed castles in Scotland, Eilean Donan sits on a small tidal island connected to the mainland by a stone bridge. After being destroyed in 1719, it was lovingly reconstructed in the 20th century and now represents the archetypal Scottish Highland castle.

Key Features
