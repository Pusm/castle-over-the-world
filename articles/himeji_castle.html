Himeji Castle

Himeji, Hyōgo Prefecture, Japan

Overview

Known as the 'White Heron Castle' for its elegant white appearance, Himeji represents the pinnacle of Japanese castle architecture. This UNESCO World Heritage site has survived 700 years virtually intact, showcasing sophisticated defensive systems and the refined aesthetic principles of Japanese design.

Castle Information

Architectural Style:
Japanese Medieval (Azuchi-Momoyama period)
Year Built:
1333 (rebuilt 1601-1609)
Location:
Himeji, Hyōgo Prefecture, Japan

Key Features

  • Network of 83 interconnected rooms
  • Advanced defensive maze systems
  • Traditional wooden construction
  • White plastered walls resembling heron wings
  • Complex multi-story design
  • Survived WWII bombing and earthquakes
