Overview

Country: Ethiopia

Location: Gondar, Amhara Region

Architectural Style: Ethiopian imperial architecture (Arab, Nubian, Gothic blend)

Year Built: 17th-18th centuries

The fortress-city of Fasil Ghebbi in Gondar was founded by Emperor Fasilides and served as the residence of Ethiopian emperors. This unique architectural complex blends Arab, Nubian, and Gothic styles, representing the cosmopolitan nature of the Ethiopian Empire.

Key Features

  • Former fortress-city and imperial residence
  • Blend of Arab, Nubian, and Gothic architecture
  • Founded by Emperor Fasilides
  • Reflects diverse Ethiopian Empire cultures
  • Complex of castles and palaces
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979
