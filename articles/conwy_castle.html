Overview

Country: Wales

Location: Conwy, North Wales

Architectural Style: 13th-century military architecture

Year Built: 1283-1289

Built by Edward I during his conquest of Wales, Conwy Castle stands as one of the finest examples of 13th-century military architecture in Europe. With its eight massive towers and high curtain walls, it forms part of Edward I's 'iron ring' of castles designed to control Wales.

Key Features

  • Eight massive round towers
  • High curtain walls
  • Finest example of 13th-century military architecture
  • Part of Edward I's 'iron ring' of Welsh castles
  • Medieval town walls integration
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
