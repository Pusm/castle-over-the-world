Conwy Castle

Conwy, North Wales, Wales

1283-1289

Overview

Built by Edward I during his conquest of Wales, Conwy Castle stands as one of the finest examples of 13th-century military architecture in Europe. With its eight massive towers and high curtain walls, it forms part of Edward I's 'iron ring' of castles designed to control Wales.
