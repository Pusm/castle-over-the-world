Alcázar of Segovia

Segovia, Castile and León, Spain

Overview

Rising dramatically from a rocky crag, the Alcázar of Segovia showcases the layered history of medieval Spain. Built on Roman foundations and expanded by Moorish, then Christian rulers, this distinctive bow-shaped castle-palace represents the cultural synthesis that defined medieval Iberia.

Castle Information

Architectural Style:
Moorish, Gothic, and Renaissance
Year Built:
12th century (on Roman/Moorish foundations)
Location:
Segovia, Castile and León, Spain

Key Features

  • Distinctive bow-shaped design
  • Throne Room with Mudéjar ceiling
  • Torre del Homenaje (Keep Tower)
  • Royal apartments with period furnishings
  • Strategic hilltop position
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
