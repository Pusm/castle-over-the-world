Overview

Country: France

Location: Blois, Loir-et-Cher, Loire Valley

Architectural Style: French Renaissance and Gothic

Year Built: 13th-17th centuries

The residence of several French kings, Château de Blois showcases four distinct architectural styles in a single complex: Gothic, Flamboyant Gothic, Renaissance, and Classical. This royal château witnessed some of the most dramatic events in French history including royal murders and political intrigue.

Key Features

  • Four distinct architectural styles
  • Residence of several French kings
  • Site of dramatic historical events
  • Famous spiral staircase
  • Royal murders and political intrigue
  • Sound and light show
