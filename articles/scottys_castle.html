Overview

Country: United States

Location: Death Valley, California

Architectural Style: Mission Revival and Spanish Colonial Revival

Year Built: 1922-1931

Despite its name, this Spanish Colonial Revival villa with two tall towers was actually built by wealthy couple Albert and Bessie Johnson as a vacation home. The legend of 'Death Valley Scotty' claiming ownership created one of America's most colorful castle stories.

Key Features

  • Mission Revival architectural style

  • Death Valley location
  • Colorful legend of Death Valley Scotty
  • Built by Albert and Bessie Johnson
