Overview

Country: India

Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan

Architectural Style: Rajput and Mughal architecture

Year Built: 1592

Perched on a hilltop overlooking Maota Lake, Amber Fort is a magnificent example of Rajput architecture blended with Mughal influences. Known for its artistic Hindu-style elements and stunning mirror work in the Sheesh Mahal, it represents the golden age of Rajasthani palace architecture.

Key Features

  • Hilltop location overlooking Maota Lake
  • Blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture
  • Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace)
  • Artistic Hindu-style elements
  • Elephant rides to entrance
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
