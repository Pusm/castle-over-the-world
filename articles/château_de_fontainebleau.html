Château de Fontainebleau

← Back to Castle Index

Overview

Country: France

Location: Fontainebleau, Seine-et-Marne

Architectural Style: French Renaissance and Classical

Year Built: 1101-1200

The only royal and imperial château continuously inhabited for seven centuries, Fontainebleau served as a residence for French monarchs from Louis VII to Napoleon III. This UNESCO site showcases the evolution of French decorative arts and architecture across multiple periods.

Key Features

  • Continuously inhabited for seven centuries
  • Residence from Louis VII to Napoleon III
  • Evolution of French decorative arts
  • Gallery of Francis I
  • Napoleon's throne room
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1981
