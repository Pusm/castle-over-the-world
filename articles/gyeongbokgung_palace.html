Overview

Country: South Korea

Location: Seoul, Jongno District

Architectural Style: Korean traditional palace architecture

Year Built: 1395

The largest of the Five Grand Palaces built during the Joseon Dynasty, Gyeongbokgung served as the main royal palace for over 500 years. Famous for its changing of the guard ceremony and beautiful pavilions set around courtyards, it represents the pinnacle of Korean royal architecture.

Key Features

  • Largest of Five Grand Palaces
  • Main royal palace of Joseon Dynasty
  • Changing of the Guard ceremony
  • Beautiful pavilions and courtyards
  • Throne Hall (Geunjeongjeon)
  • Traditional Korean architectural elements
