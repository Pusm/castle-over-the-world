Château de Chambord

Loire Valley, Loir-et-Cher, France

Overview

A masterpiece of French Renaissance architecture, Chambord showcases the architectural revolution that swept France in the 16th century. With its distinctive double-helix staircase possibly designed by Leonardo da Vinci, this château represents the perfect fusion of French medieval traditions with Italian Renaissance innovation.

Castle Information

Architectural Style:
French Renaissance
Year Built:
1519-1547
Location:
Loire Valley, Loir-et-Cher, France

Key Features

  • 440 rooms and 365 fireplaces
  • Double-helix staircase (possibly da Vinci design)
  • Distinctive French Renaissance silhouette
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1981
  • Innovative roof terraces and towers
  • Largest château in Loire Valley
