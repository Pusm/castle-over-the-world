Overview

Country: Zimbabwe

Location: Masvingo Province, Zimbabwe

Architectural Style: African stone architecture

Year Built: 11th-15th centuries

The largest ancient structure south of the Sahara Desert, Great Zimbabwe was the capital of the Kingdom of Zimbabwe. These impressive stone walls built without mortar represent the pinnacle of indigenous African architecture and gave the modern country its name.

Key Features

  • Largest ancient structure south of Sahara
  • Built without mortar (dry stone technique)
  • Capital of Kingdom of Zimbabwe
  • Gave Zimbabwe its name
  • Complex of stone enclosures
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1986
