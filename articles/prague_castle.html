Prague Castle

Prague, Hradčany, Czech Republic

9th century (continuously expanded)

Overview

According to Guinness World Records, Prague Castle is the largest ancient castle complex in the world, spanning nearly 70,000 square meters. This millennium-spanning architectural marvel reflects the changing styles and powers that have shaped Central Europe, from Bohemian kings to Habsburg emperors.

Basic Information

Architectural Style:
Romanesque, Gothic, Renaissance, Baroque blend
Construction Period:
9th century (continuously expanded)
Location:
Prague, Hradčany, Czech Republic

Key Features & Highlights

  • Largest ancient castle complex (70,000 sq meters)
  • St. Vitus Cathedral with Gothic spires
  • Old Royal Palace with Vladislav Hall
  • Renaissance and Baroque additions
  • Official residence of Czech presidents
  • Architectural timeline of 1000+ years
